I. Objective  
The objective of this task is to implement an interactive Eisenhower Matrix Module  within qTASK, referencing the UI/UX 
layout of TickTick. This feature will automatically map and display tasks into four distinct quadrants based on their urgency  
(Target Date) and importance (Severity), allowing users to immediately identify high -priority  work.  
 
 
II. Specifications  
A. Frontend & User Interface (UI) Requirements  
The OJT team must build a new tab view titled "Eisenhower Matrix" that splits the screen into a $2 \times 2$ Grid 
Interface . 
 
A. The 4 Quadrants Layout  
1. Quadrant I: Urgent & Important (Do First) [Top -Left]:  For tasks requiring immediate action.  
2. Quadrant II: Not Urgent  & Important (Schedule) [Top -Right]:  For long -term goals and strategic planning.  
3. Quadrant III: Urgent & Not Important (Delegate) [Bottom -Left]:  For operational tasks that need quick 
completion but low complexity.  
4. Quadrant IV: Not Urgent & Not Important (Eliminate) [Bottom -Right]:  For low -priority backlog tasks.  
 
B. View Controls & Filtering Strip  
Directly above the 2 x 2 grid, include a control bar similar to the Master Grid to adjust the scope of the matrix view:  
• [ Select Project: ALL / Specific v ]  
• [ Select Assignee: ALL / Specific v ] (Allows PMs to see the matrix of individual OJTs, or allows devs to look at their 
own matrix).

B. Backend Mapping & Logic Rules  
To prevent users from having to manually assign quadrants, the system must automatically categorize tasks  by 
cross -referencing two existing database parameters: Severity  (Importance) and Target Date  (Urgency).  
 
The backend API must filter and distribute tasks using the following matrix logic:  
Quadrant  Importance Condition (Severity)  Urgency Condition (Target Date)  
Q1: Do First  CRITICAL / SHOWSTOPPER OR HIGH  Due Today, Past Due, or Due within 48 Hours  
Q2: Schedule  CRITICAL / SHOWSTOPPER OR HIGH  Due later than 48 Hours / No immediate deadline  
Q3: Delegate  MEDIUM OR LOW  Due Today, Past Due, or Due within 48 Hours  
Q4: Eliminate  MEDIUM OR LOW  No immediate deadline / Long -term Backlog  
 
 
 
C. Technical Stack Recommendations  
• CSS Grid / Flexbox:  Utilize modern CSS Grid layouts to ensure the 2 x 2 quadrant split remains fully responsive 
across screens.  
• Drag -and -Drop API:  Use lightweight sorting libraries like vuedraggable , react -beautiful -dnd , or SortableJS  
(depending on qTASK's frontend stack) to manage card moving between the quadrants cleanly.  
 
 
III. Layout Reference  
The development team may  study and closely emulate TickTick’s Eisenhower Matrix layout  to guide their 
implementation:  🎯 Eisenhower Matrix  
• Quadrants Grid Symmetry:  Follow TickTick’s minimalist $2 \times 2$ box layout. Each quadrant must have its own 
scrollbar so a high volume of tasks doesn't break the uniform sizing of the screen grid.  
• Drag -and -Drop Matrix Adjustments:  Copy TickTick's drag mechanics. If a PM or developer drags a task card out of 
Quadrant II (Schedule)  and drops it into Quadrant I (Do First) , the system must automatically update that task's 
database record (e.g., automatically bumping its Severity to HIGH or moving its Target Date to Today).  
• Quick -Add Field per Quadrant:  Emulate TickTick's input field located inside or at the top of each individual quadrant 
box, allowing a manager to rapidly type a task name and hit Enter to create a task pre -mapped directly to that 
quadrant.

IV. Definition of Done (DoD)  
This module shall be considered officially complete when:  
1. The Eisenhower Matrix splits cleanly into 4 uniform, scrollable quadrants.  
2. Tasks from the database are accurately mapped into their respective quadrants based on the Severity and Target Date 
logic table.  
3. Manually dragging a task from one quadrant to another successfully mutates and saves the new Severity/Target Date 
payload in the database.  
4. The matrix layout, text font density, and quadrant colors perfectly match the polished efficiency of TickTick's 
prioritization matrix view.
